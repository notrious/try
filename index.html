Bhutan The Land Of Happiness

Bhutan is a tiny and remote kingdom nestling in the Himalayas between its powerful neighbours, India and China. Almost completely cut off for centuries, it has tried to let in some aspects of the outside world while fiercely guarding its ancient traditions

Bhutanese culture is very much influenced by sacred Buddhist values. Gho and KIRA

THE NATIONAL DRESS OF BHUTAN


Gho

HOW TO WEAR GHO?

The national dress of Bbhutan is called the Gho for men and Kira for women. It was introduced during the 17th century by Zhabdrung Ngawang Namgyel to give the Bhhutanese a unique identity. The Gho is a long robe hoisted to the knee and held in place with a Kira, a woven cloth belt, wound tighty around the waist.

Kira

HOW TO WEAR KIRA?

The dress popular along the Bhutanese womenflok is called the kira. It is ankle-length and is basically a rectangular piece of cloth that wraps around the body. Bhutanese women either wear a full kira (like a dress) or a half kira (like a skirt). The kira is fastened at the shoulders by complicated silver hooks called koma, and at the waist with a belt that could either silver or cloth.

National sport

National Game Archery.

HISTORY ABOUT ARCHERY

Archery in Bhutan is culturally distinctive because it is a martial art practiced among a modern population that abhors killing. Bhutanese people from different social strata find archery one of the most enjoyable sports, being both fun and physical exercise.Declared as the national sport in 1971, the inclination of people towards archery in Bhutan started in the 1920s, under the leadership of Druk Gyalpo Jigme Wangchuk, the second king of the country. However, archery has been practised in the country since long before then.

Rules and Tournaments

There are usually two teams in every match, each of them having their own supporters, who cheer for them and motivate them throughout the tournament. Participants usually wear a bright red or orange-coloured uniform during competitions. In traditional matches, thigh-length socks and a robe are also carried. When an arrow hits the board just close to the centre mark, one point is given to him. For two points, the arrow has to hit the central circle and when the arrow hits the bird's eye, 3 points are marked. With every point being earned, the cheerleaders dance and sing the traditional songs and encourage the participants. To lower down the confidence of the opponent team, constants hootings and booing are also the part of the match. And being a part of this crazy enthusiasm is an experience in itself! No major festival or celebrations are complete without a match of archery in which the entire village comes together to enjoy a marvellous match

Role of Women in Archer

Women's role in archery is very important as they make for the supporting people, whose presence doubles the thrill of the tournament.They bring food for them and dance in joy every time their team hits the target and sing traditional songs to cheer up their own team. This is a great source of motivation for the players, who are being expected to win the match
